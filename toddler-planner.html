Learning Tips for 3-Year-Olds
1
Keep sessions short — 10-15 min per activity. A 3yo's attention span is about 6-15 minutes.
2
Play-based learning — use songs, games, flashcards with pictures, and physical movement.
3
Repetition is key — review previously learned items at the start of each session.
4
Multi-sensory approach — trace letters in sand, form characters with playdough, count real objects.
5
Follow the child's interest — if they engage more with numbers today, extend that activity.
6
Celebrate small wins — stickers, high-fives, and praise reinforce positive learning attitudes.

Bedtime Routine Tips
1
Consistent wind-down: Start the same routine 45 min before target bedtime — dim lights, quiet activities.
2
No screens for at least 1 hour before bed. Blue light delays melatonin by 30+ minutes.
3
Physical activity during the day — playground and swimming help tremendously. Keep the 5-6:30 PM active play.
4
Cool, dark room — set AC to 24-25°C. Use blackout curtains if possible.
5
Gradual shift: Move bedtime 15 min earlier every 3-4 days. Don't rush — sudden changes cause resistance.
6
Books before bed: Reading 2-3 short books signals "sleep is coming." Let him pick the books.
7
Avoid sugar after dinner. Even fruit juice can give an energy spike. Water or warm milk are better.
8
Comfort object: A favorite stuffed animal or blanket provides security and self-soothing.
9
White noise or soft music: Helps block out household sounds. Play at low volume consistently.
10
Sticker chart: Reward good bedtime behavior. 5 stickers = a small prize. Positive reinforcement works!
